At a glance

Improv Cheat Sheet

The rules, patterns, and concepts from all six modules — all in one place.

The fundamentals

The 5 Rules of Improv

  1. 1 Say "yes, and." Accept what your partner gives you and add something new.
  2. 2 Support your partner. Make the people around you look good.
  3. 3 There are no mistakes. Everything becomes part of the scene.
  4. 4 Commit fully. If you're going to do it, do it all the way.
  5. 5 Listen more than you talk. Pay attention to what's actually happening.

Scene work

Starting a Scene

Establish three things as quickly as you can:

  • Who are you to each other? (friends, strangers, siblings, aliens)
  • Where are you? (show it or say it)
  • What is happening? (something needs to be going on)

Character work

Building a Character

Pick one or more of these and commit:

  • Body: Change something physical and let everything else follow
  • Attitude: Pick one strong emotion or personality trait and crank it up
  • Want: Give your character something they want right now in this scene

When you're stuck

Things to Add When You Don't Know What to Say

  • A detail about the environment ("It smells like burned popcorn in here.")
  • How your character feels ("I'm kind of nervous about this.")
  • A new piece of information ("We have to be there in five minutes.")

Watch out for these

Don't Do These

  • ✕ Block: Don't reject what your partner just established
  • ✕ Ask too many questions: Questions put the work on your partner. Make statements instead.
  • ✕ Plan ahead: If you're thinking about your next line, you're not listening
  • ✕ Play it safe: The person who goes all in always gets a bigger reaction than the person who holds back

Comedy patterns

The Patterns That Make Things Funny

  • Rule of three: Set up a pattern with two normal things, break it with a third unexpected thing
  • Escalation: Whatever just happened, make the next thing bigger
  • Callback: Reference something from earlier in the scene in a new context
  • Specificity: "A 1997 minivan with a bumper sticker that said 'my other car is also terrible'" is always funnier than "a car"

Timing

Timing Tips

  • The pause: Sometimes the funniest thing you can do is nothing. Let the moment land.
  • The reaction: The biggest laughs often come from reacting to the funny thing, not doing the funny thing
  • Know when to stop talking: A look between two characters can say more than a paragraph of dialogue

Sketch writing

Sketch Comedy Structure

  1. Setup: Establish who, where, and what — fast
  2. Introduce the game: The one central funny idea this sketch is built around
  3. Heighten: Escalate the game. Each beat is bigger than the last.
  4. Button: End on a laugh. Get out before the energy drops.

Before you go on

  • Trust your partner
  • Trust yourself
  • Be willing to look ridiculous
  • Have fun
